LA ARAÑA

'La araña es una araña enorme que ya no anda; una araña incolora, cuyo cuerpo, una cabeza y un abdomen, sangra.'

the tiny spider traps you, it catches you and webs you, it doesnt let you breath until you finally desease.Silken threads, a shimmering snare, Entwining hope with whispered despair. In shadows cast, it weaves its art, A master of patience, a cunning heart.With each gentle pull, your struggles fade, As it dances close, in the dark parade. A predator veiled in delicate grace, In its intricate web, you find no space. Eyes like embers, they glimmer and gleam, Reflecting your fears, extinguishing dreams. A flick of a leg, a silken embrace, Your will wanes beneath the lace. You thought you were free, a wanderer bold, But the spider's embrace is a story retold. With venomous whispers, it lures you near, In this fragile trap, you succumb to fear.
